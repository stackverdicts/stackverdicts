üìñ Introduction & Mindset

The Golden Rule of YouTube Keyword Research

Find the intersection of three things:

  1. What people are actively searching for (demand)
  2. What you can realistically rank for (competition level)
  3. What aligns with your monetization goals (hosting products)

Understanding the YouTube Search Ecosystem

YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine. Unlike Google, people come to YouTube specifically for:

  • Visual demonstrations - "How to set up WordPress hosting"
  • Comparisons - "Bluehost vs SiteGround 2025"
  • Reviews - "Is Kinsta worth it?"
  • Tutorials - "Deploy website to VPS step by step"
  • Problem-solving - "Website running slow how to fix"

üí° Mindset Shift

Don't just think about "keywords" - think about questions your ideal viewer is asking. Every successful hosting affiliate video solves a specific problem or answers a specific question.

üéì Keyword Research Fundamentals

The Three Pillars of Keyword Opportunity

1. Search Volume

What it means: How many people are searching for this topic

Sweet spot: 1K-100K monthly searches

Why: High enough demand, not oversaturated

2. Competition

üõ†Ô∏è Essential Free Tools

Your YouTube Keyword Research Toolkit

1. YouTube Search Bar (Built-in Autocomplete)

Why it's powerful: Shows you EXACTLY what people are typing into YouTube right now

How to use:

  1. Open YouTube in incognito mode (prevents personalized results)
  2. Type your base keyword: web hosting
  3. Add a space and each letter of the alphabet: web hosting a, web hosting b, etc.
  4. Note all suggestions - these are real searches
  5. Try modifiers: "best", "how to", "vs", "review", "tutorial", "for beginners"

2. YouTube Search Results Analysis

What to look for:

  • View counts on top 10 videos
  • Upload dates (recent or old?)
  • Channel sizes (can you compete?)
  • Video quality/thumbnails
  • Comment counts (engagement level)
  • Are top videos even answering the query well?

3. Google Trends (trends.google.com)

Use for:

  • Seasonal trends (hosting deals around Black Friday)
  • Rising vs declining topics
  • Regional interest
  • Related queries

Pro tip: Set the filter to "YouTube Search" instead of "Web Search"

4. Answer The Public (answerthepublic.com)

Free searches per day: Limited but valuable

Use for: Finding question-based keywords

  • What, Why, Where, When, Who, How questions
  • Prepositions (hosting for, hosting with, hosting without)
  • Comparisons (hosting vs, hosting versus)

5. YouTube Analytics - Your Own Data

Once you have videos:

  • Check "Traffic sources" > "YouTube search"
  • See what search terms are ALREADY bringing people to you
  • Create more videos around these proven keywords
  • Look for unexpected keywords you're ranking for

6. Competitor Channel Analysis

Find successful hosting channels:

  1. Search for broad hosting terms
  2. Find channels with 10K-500K subscribers (achievable range)
  3. Sort their videos by "Most popular"
  4. Note which videos performed best
  5. Analyze their titles and topics
  6. Find gaps you can fill

üîç Finding Keyword Opportunities

Method 1: The Alphabet Soup Technique

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Start with base keyword: web hosting
  2. Add each letter:
    • web hosting a ‚Üí "web hosting affiliate program"

  3. Try prefixes:

  4. Add year: web hosting 2025
  5. Add qualifiers:
    • web hosting for beginners
    • web hosting for small business

Method 2: Question Mining

High-Intent Question Starters

Combine your topic with these question words:

How questions (Tutorial intent):

  • "how to choose web hosting"
  • "how to migrate to new host"
  • "how to set up WordPress hosting"

What questions (Information intent):

  • "what is the best hosting for WordPress"
  • "what hosting does [famous site] use"
  • "what is shared hosting vs VPS"

Which questions (Comparison intent):

  • "which web host is the fastest"
  • "which hosting is best for ecommerce"
  • "which hosting to choose for blog"

Why questions (Explanation intent):

  • "why is my website slow"
  • "why managed WordPress hosting"
  • "why is Kinsta so expensive"

Method 3: Problem-Solution Mining

Find Problems People Have

Search on YouTube for these problem patterns:

  • "hosting [problem]" - "hosting too slow"
  • "website [problem]" - "website keeps going down"
  • "WordPress [problem]" - "WordPress site hacked"
  • "can't [action]" - "can't install SSL certificate"
  • "[service] not working" - "email hosting not working"
Example: Someone searching "why is my WordPress site slow" is likely to buy better hosting if you show them the solution!

Method 4: Competitive Gap Analysis

Finding What Competitors Missed

  1. Search your main keyword: web hosting
  2. Analyze top 20 results: What topics do they cover?
  3. Find the gaps:
    • Topics mentioned but not deeply covered
    • Outdated videos (2+ years old)
    • Poor production quality you can beat
    • Missing perspectives (budget, specific use case, etc.)
  4. Create better content that fills these gaps

Method 5: Related Searches Mining

The Rabbit Hole Technique

  1. Search your keyword on YouTube
  2. Scroll to the bottom of search results
  3. Note "Related searches" or suggested videos in sidebar
  4. Click one of those searches
  5. Repeat - document all new keywords you find
  6. After 5-10 iterations, you'll have dozens of keyword ideas

‚öñÔ∏è Evaluating Keyword Potential

The ABCDE Framework for Keyword Evaluation

A - Audience Size (Search Volume)

How to estimate manually:

  • Search the keyword on YouTube
  • Look at views on top 10 videos
  • If top videos have 10K+ views and are months/years old = good demand
  • If top videos have <1K views = low demand (unless very new)

Ideal range: Top 3 videos have combined 50K+ views

Good sign: #1 video: 100K views, #2: 50K views, #3: 30K views
Warning sign: #1 video: 2K views, #2: 800 views, #3: 300 views

B - Beatable Competition

Analyze top-ranking videos:

Factor Can You Beat It? Score
Channel size <50K subs Yes! You can compete +2 points
Video older than 2 years Yes! Create updated version +2 points
Poor video quality Yes! Invest in better production +2 points
Low engagement (few comments) Yes! Encourage discussion +1 point
Doesn't fully answer query Yes! Be more comprehensive +2 points

Scoring:

  • 7+ points = GO FOR IT (low competition)
  • 4-6 points = CONSIDER IT (medium competition)
  • 0-3 points = AVOID (high competition)

C - Commercial Intent

Does this keyword indicate buying intent?

HIGH commercial intent keywords:

  • "best hosting for [use case]"
  • "[Brand A] vs [Brand B]"
  • "[Brand] review"
  • "cheapest hosting"
  • "how to choose hosting"

MEDIUM commercial intent:

  • "what is web hosting"
  • "how does hosting work"
  • "types of web hosting"

LOW commercial intent (avoid):

  • "history of web hosting"
  • "web hosting memes"
  • Entertainment-focused queries

D - Difficulty Score

Manual difficulty assessment:

  1. Search keyword on YouTube
  2. Analyze top 10 videos:
    • Average channel size
    • Average views per video
    • Video quality level
    • How long they've ranked
  3. Calculate rough difficulty:
    • Easy: Mostly small channels, older videos, lower quality
    • Medium: Mix of small and medium channels, decent quality
    • Hard: Mostly large channels, high production value, recent

E - Evergreen Value

Will this keyword stay relevant?

EVERGREEN (best):

  • "how to choose web hosting"
  • "shared hosting vs VPS explained"
  • "WordPress hosting guide"

SEASONAL (good but time-sensitive):

  • "best hosting deals Black Friday 2025"
  • "New hosting features 2025"

üí° Pro Strategy

Create 80% evergreen content and 20% seasonal/trending content. Evergreen videos generate views for years, while seasonal content captures short-term spikes.

The 10-Point Validation Checklist

  • Search volume check: Top 3 videos have combined 30K+ views?
  • Competition check: Can you beat at least 3 of the top 10 videos?
  • Recency check: Are top videos older than 1 year?
  • Intent check: Does this keyword indicate buying intent?
  • Relevance check: Can you naturally promote hosting in this video?
  • Channel size check: Are top channels under 100K subs?
  • Quality check: Is there room for better production value?
  • Answer quality check: Do existing videos fully answer the query?
  • Trending check: Is interest growing or stable? (Google Trends)
  • Profitability check: Can you make affiliate commissions from this topic?

    4-5 checks passed: MAYBE - Consider if you have unique angle

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Mistake #1: Only targeting high-volume keywords

    Why it's wrong: High volume = high competition. You'll never rank.

    Better approach: Target 80% medium/low volume keywords where you can actually rank, 20% aspirational high-volume keywords.

    Mistake #2: Ignoring search intent

    Why it's wrong: Someone searching "what is web hosting" isn't ready to buy. Someone searching "Bluehost vs SiteGround" is.

    Better approach: Focus on keywords with clear buying intent (comparisons, reviews, "best" lists).

    Mistake #3: Creating content you CAN'T monetize

    Why it's wrong: Views without conversions = wasted effort.

    Better approach: Every keyword should have a clear path to your affiliate links. If you can't naturally promote hosting, skip it.

    Mistake #4: Not considering your competitive position

    Why it's wrong: A new channel can't compete with established creators on popular topics.

    Better approach: Start with niche, specific keywords. As you grow, tackle broader terms.

    Mistake #5: Chasing trends instead of evergreen topics

    Why it's wrong: Trending topics die fast. Last year's "hot hosting deal" gets zero views today.

    Better approach: Build foundation with evergreen content. Add seasonal content as bonuses.

    Mistake #6: Not tracking your results

    Why it's wrong: You'll keep making the same mistakes.

    Better approach: Use a spreadsheet to track:

    • Keyword targeted
    • Video performance (views, CTR, watch time)
    • Ranking position
    • Affiliate clicks/conversions

    Mistake #7: Giving up too soon

    Why it's wrong: Videos can take 3-6 months to gain traction.

    Better approach: Give each video at least 6 months. Optimize with new thumbnails, titles if needed. Some of your "failures" will become top performers over time.
